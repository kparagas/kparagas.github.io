Characterizing hot, rocky exoplanets

Close-in, rocky planets orbiting M dwarfs are ideal targets for potential atmosphere and surface characterization. With JWST's Mid-InfraRed Instrument (MIRI), we can leverage low-resolution spectroscopy to search for spectral signatures in a planet's thermal emission.

I'm currently developing a (soon-to-be public) framework for simultaneously retrieving atmosphere and surface properties of these planets on their secondary eclipse measurements.

Probing atmospheric escape with the helium (He) I triplet

I've previously worked on detecting the extended 1083 nm He I atmosphere of the gas giant HAT-P-18 b using an ultra-narrow band filter installed on the 200" Hale Telescope at Palomar Observatory.
